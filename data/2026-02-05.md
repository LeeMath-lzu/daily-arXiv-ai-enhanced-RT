<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 研究线性Nakayama代数（上三角矩阵代数的商代数）的配置空间和多面体的组合结构，为更一般代数的配置空间理论提供具体特例的显式构造。

Method: 从Dyck路径出发，构造三个相关对象：有限维代数、仿射代数簇和多面体。使用初等证明和显式组合构造，建立Dyck路径偏序集中的关系与对应对象间态射的自然对应。

Result: 建立了Dyck路径与线性Nakayama代数配置空间之间的显式组合对应，证明了这些构造的自然性：Dyck路径偏序集中的每个关系都诱导相应对象之间的态射。

Abstract: We present a combinatorial model of configuration spaces and polytopes associated to the quotients of $\mathbb{C} A_n$, the path algebra of the linearly oriented $A_n$ quiver, i.e. the algebra of upper triangular matrices. These quotient algebras are known as linear Nakayama algebras. Such configuration spaces were recently introduced for more general algebras by the second author and collaborators. In this special setting, we provide elementary proofs and explicit combinatorial constructions. From a Dyck path we define three related objects: a finite-dimensional algebra, an affine algebraic variety, and a polytope. Moreover, our constructions are natural: each relation in the poset of Dyck paths gives a morphism between the corresponding objects.

  <summary>Details</summary>

### [7] [On The Relative Cohomology For Algebraic Groups](https://arxiv.org/abs/2505.00833)
*Gabriel T. Loos*

Main category: math.GR

TL;DR: 本文扩展了Kimura关于代数群相对上同调的工作，建立了相对上同调的基础理论，包括相对正合序列、相对内射模的定义和性质，并证明了相对Grothendieck谱序列的存在条件。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: Hochschild在1961年定义了代数群的上同调群Ext_G^i(M,N)，Kimura在1965年将其推广到相对上同调。虽然原始上同调群在理解代数群表示理论中很重要，但相对上同调的作用仍不清楚。本文旨在建立相对上同调的基础理论。

Method: 作者首先定义了相对正合序列和相对内射模，并证明了它们的基本性质。特别地，归纳函子在研究相对内射模中起重要作用。在奠定必要基础后，给出了相对上同调的定义，并建立了相对Grothendieck谱序列。

Result: 建立了相对上同调的完整理论基础，包括相对正合序列和相对内射模的性质，给出了相对上同调的明确定义，并证明了相对Grothendieck谱序列的存在条件，提供了许多推论和例子。

Conclusion: 本文成功扩展了Kimura关于代数群相对上同调的工作，建立了系统的基础理论框架，为深入研究相对上同调在代数群表示理论中的作用奠定了基础。

Abstract: Let $G$ be an algebraic group over a field $k$, and $M$ and $N$ be $G$-modules. In 1961, Hochschild showed how one can define the cohomology groups $\text{Ext}_{G}^{i}(M,N)$. Kimura, in 1965, showed that one can generalize this to get relative cohomology for algebraic groups. The original cohomology groups play an important role in understanding the representation theory of $G$, but the role of relative cohomology is still not well understood.
  In this paper the author expands upon the work of Kimura to prove foundational results about the relative cohomology. The author starts by giving the definitions of relative exact sequences and relative injective modules and proves a variety of basic properties for each that will be essential to define relative cohomology and obtain a relative Grothendieck spectral sequence. In particular, the induction functor will play an important role when studying the relative injective modules. Once the necessary ground work is laid, the definition of relative cohomology is given. Finally, it is stated when there is a relative Grothendieck spectral sequence, and many consequences and examples are provided.

</details>
